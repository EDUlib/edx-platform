2. Subject

EDUlib was launched by HEC Montréal in October 2012 with the goal of making first-rate French-language university education in the field of management available to the greatest number of people. EDUlib is now a joint initiative of Université de Montréal and its two affiliated schools, HEC Montréal and Polytechnique Montréal, the founding members. While the goal remains the same, our offering has expanded to include courses in the fields of expertise of the three partners. EDUlib courses are modeled on face-to-face instruction: they are taught by the same professors, and their content is of a university calibre. Course format and frequency may change, but the quality of our teaching does not.

3. Rights and Responsibilities of Persons Registered on EDUlib

3.1 Privacy Policy

By registering on EDUlib, you agree to provide your first name, last name and a valid email as well as a unique identifier. You also agree to allow participating institutions to create a file containing your personal information. This information is confidential. It will be used for the purpose of creating your user file, which will be kept in accordance with the retention schedule adopted under the provisions of the Quebec Archives Act. Information collected is compulsory in nature, and anyone who refuses to provide it will be prevented from registering on EDUlib.

You have the right to request access to your file and have any inaccurate, incomplete, or ambiguous information corrected. By registering on EDUlib and engaging in EDUlib activities, the participant undertakes to comply with the provisions of the law, statutes, regulations, resolutions, policies, guidelines, and practices of the participating institutions.

Any falsified document, false statement, or failure to provide required information can result in cancellation of a user's EDUlib account.

You also authorize participating institutions to contact you by email at the address provided in the registration form, so they may inform you about various activities and products.

3.2 Data Anonymization for Research Purposes

Within the EDUlib course framework, authorized research teams collect and analyze the digital footprints of user activity in order to generate new scientific knowledge and improve course content. The digital footprints to which researchers have access are anonymized using a computer process that ensures all personal data by which a user can be identified is removed and their identity is irreversibly altered

3.5 Use of Cookies

Some information is gathered through cookies, small text files stored on your computer that collect information about you and can be consulted by our webmasters.

A cookie does not identify you. Generally speaking, it records information about how you navigate the EDUlib site (the pages you visit, the date and time, etc.). This enables the site to access the cookie’s data during your subsequent visits. In this instance, it contains information you have already provided. That way you won't need to complete certain forms each time your visit.

You can refuse to accept the cookies via the preferences in your browser. Most browsers provide instructions on how to disable cookies in the “Help” section of their toolbar. The browser settings allow you to adjust your cookie permissions to block some and make exceptions for others. However, if you choose to disable cookies, certain features of the website might not work properly.

4. Certificates

EDUlib and its partner institutions who offer courses via the website can provide a certificate to students who have demonstrated mastery of the course material. Certificates are issued by the institution and the professor in charge of the course. The decision as to whether or not to issue a certificate is at the sole discretion of the institution and the professor. A fee may or may not be charged, also at the discretion of institution and the professor. This is will be indicated in the "About" section of the course. Even when a fee is charged for the certificate, access to the course remains free.

4.1 Certificates and Identity Verification

Certificates are issued in the name given by the participant upon registering for the course.

Paid certificates will be issued once the participant's name and identity have been verified in one of the following ways: previously verified email address, a photo of the participant, photo ID issued by a government or other approved means of reference.

4.2 Payment for Certificates

Some courses charge a fee for receiving a certificate, but access to the course is still free. If your identity has not been previously verified, the following steps will be taken in regards to your registration:

  • You will be asked to submit a photo and some form of identification. These two pieces of ID will be later validated.
  • The certificate fee will be charged to your credit card by a third-party service provider.
  • Once the transaction is completed, you will be redirected to the EDUlib site, and you will receive an email confirming your name, order number, and the amount paid. Please keep this information for any future refund requests.

4.3 Refund Policy

You may drop a course and request a refund within 14 calendar days of the start of the course (grace period). To do so, click the "Unsubscribe" link on the course page in your dashboard and send a request for a refund by email to facturation@edulib.org. Include your name and purchase order number in the email. Refunds will be credited to the credit card you used when you placed the order and can take up to two payment cycles to be processed. EDUlib is under no obligation to issue a refund after the grace period has expired.

4.4 Identity Verification

In order to verify your identity, and even after registration, you may be asked to take a selfie and a photo of one of your ID cards. Access to this information is restricted in accordance with EDUlib's privacy policy.

The following ID documents are eligible:

  • Driver's license
  • Passport
  • Government-issued photo ID

In order to be accepted, your ID must:

  • Contain the same first and last names as the ones you used to register on EDUlib;
  • Be current (less than 10 years old);
  • Be valid.

If these criteria are not met, EDUlib cannot validate your identity or provide you with authorized certificates.
